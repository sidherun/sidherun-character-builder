# Dice Rolling
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

Most rolls conducted by the GM and players in Sidherun will be with two, ten-sided dice, representing the numbers 01-100 (01-00). In almost all cases, the higher the number, the greater the outcome. The target of all rolls is determined by the GM, taking into effect various criteria. The goal of any player's roles will be to **match or beat the target assigned by the GM**

- Example(s)
  - Richard wants to roll to see if he can lift a medium sized stone from atop a small grave which his character is trying to read. The GM tells Richard he needs to beat a target of 50 (easy roll) to successfully lift the stone

For all rolls, a player may add the appropriate attribute (assigned by GM) to **add to their roll and assist in their success**

- Example(s):
  - Richard wants to roll to see if he can move a large stone from the entrance to a small cave which his character and the rest of their party are trying to enter. The GM tells Richard he needs to beat a target of 90 (hard for low level characters). Richard rolls a 75 (natural) and adds his Strength modifier (15) for a total of 90. With this success, the GM tells Richard that his character has successfully rolled the stone aside.

For all rolls, the following apply: See Critical and Fumbles for more details

- **Critical Success:** Any roll resulting in a natural 96-100 (96-00) allows the player to roll again, adding the number of the first roll to any subsequent rolls
  - This can lead to multiple rolls, if a player rolls consecutive (or more) 96-100 rolls for a given action
    - Example(s)
      - Richard wants to roll to see if he can lift a large boulder that is blocking the entrance to a cave he and his party are trying to enter. The GM tells Richard that this would be very difficult indeed, as the boulder is many times Richard's size and made of dense stone. Richard wants to lift the stone to throw it down the hill at trolls which are pursuing him and the party, so he decides to try it. The GM tells Richard the boulder is larger than he can normally lift, so he assigns Richard a score of 125 (impossible without some help).
      - Richard roles a 96, a CRIT! Using the Crit rules, Richard rolls a 2nd time, rolling a 90. Adding the two scores to his Strength attribute (15), Richard's character's total is 201, a HUGE SUCCESS! The GM and player decide the boulder is lifted, thrown down the hill, striking all 4 of the pursuing trolls, sending them back down the hill, and doing significant damage!
- **Fumbled Failure:** Any roll resulting in a natural 01-05 forces the player to roll again on the fumble heat chart.

**Example Roll Difficulties**

| Difficulty | Target |
|---|---|
| Effortless | No Roll (GM Discretion) |
| Simple | 50 |
| Straightforward | 75 |
| Challenging | 100 |
| Demanding | 125 |
| Intense | 150 |
| Rigorous | 175 |
| Vexing | 200+ |
