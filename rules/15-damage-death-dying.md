# Combat Damage, Death, and Dying in Sidherun
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

In Sidherun, life is at a premium. Many creatures, big and small, are all fighting for survival in a world full of predators and prey, massive armies, grey wizards, high-flying attackers and creatures which come from nightmares. As such, it's important to protect yourself. In the game, there are many ways to do this:

- Practicing good Defensive techniques to reduce the chance to get hit in combat
- Building clever skills which help to avoid combat and/or avoid getting hit in combat
- Wearing the right amount of armor that allows a good mix of movement and damage absorption
- Being in a well-balanced party of friends that offer protection

Even when taking all the precautions above, however, characters are still going to get hit and take damage. Let's discuss how that happens:

## Taking Damage

Unless otherwise stated, damage is taken in the following order:

- Area of effect to the character's **armor**
- Personal damage to the character **armor**
- Area of effect to the character
- Personal damage to the character

Example(s)

- Martinuk and Kleg are with Ingrid as they have descended deep into a dark sewer system to find a vile night creature who has been using the sewer system to terrorize the local neighborhood above. Due to some bad luck on rolls, the 3 are surprised by a pair of spell casting Nosferatu, who both get the 'surprise' effect for one round prior to the actual combat rounds beginning.

One of the Nosferatu casts a vile, toxic cloud of stench around the 3 adventurers, while the 2nd Nosferatu launches a pair of magic stakes which can impale unsuspecting mortals, causing vast damage at the group.

For this scenario:

- Let's assume the group is, indeed, surprised
- Let's also assume the group has not equipped with any spells/powers/skills to avoid the onslaught

The order these 2 attacks would be conducted

- First, the acidic cloud, dealing 8 points of damage/round would be applied to all 3 of the characters
  - In this case, none of the characters have armor that would absorb this type of gas, so all 3 of them take 8 points of damage
  - In this case, as the toxic cloud is not acidic, none of their armor take any damage – HOWEVER – If this were an acid-based cloud, the 8 points of damage could be applied to the armor, even if it didn't prevent any damage to the characters
- Second, resolving the two magic stakes:
  - The first targets Ingrid, who has no armor, as it slows her down too much
    - Ingrid is struck by the spell, and takes a whopping 12 points of damage, as the dark stake impales her in the midsection (for a total of 20 points in this one round)
  - The 2nd targets Martinuk, who wears a suit of chain mail he recently acquired. As this stake is physical (even though it originated from a spell), the stake hits his armor first. As chain mail armor absorbs 8 points of damage, his armor takes the 8 points, and he is left unscathed. Had this targeted his partner, Kleg, who wears no armor, he would have taken the damage as well, as his incredible dodge abilities would have been no use to a surprise, spell-based attack such as this.
- This leaves us with this damage done prior to combat even starting:

| Name | Armor Dmg Taken | Character Dmg Taken |
|---|---|---|
| Ingrid | 0 (no armor worn) | 20 |
| Martinuk | 8 (absorbed by chain mail) | 8 (poison cloud) |
| Kleg | 0 (no armor worn) | 8 (poison cloud, not targeted by stakes) |

Given an average character has between 40-50 starting hit points, attention must be paid to damage avoidance wherever possible
