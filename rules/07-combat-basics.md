# Combat as a Skill:
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

In Sidherun, Combat is handled as a specialized type of skill, with additional modifiers that may or may not exist in a typical skill roll. In addition, as a combat skill implies that you are impacting another character's health, there are several conditions that must be more clearly outlined.

At its base, **Combat** is handled by a simple % die roll, adding any bonuses, attempting to beat a target number, determined by defenders' bonuses.

For both melee and ranged combat, is the same system, but we'll handle some specific scenarios in detail, to help with understanding

The concept behind this combat system is to keep the game moving at all times, while attaining some form of realism to the types of activities that characters are trying to perform. As such, **there is some math**

**The GM of Sidherun should be able to do math to assist players** *- However,* it's a clever idea to have a way to get some math done on your own behalf. You're free to:

- Do it in your head (if you are so inclined)
- Use a calculator
- Use scrap paper (if you believe in paper as a medium)
- Use a digital device on which you write

*::::::::::In all cases, however::::::::::*

*You must call out your numbers as you go and be transparent.* Role playing games have a medium of trust built into them. GM's, as well as players, should always be ready to talk about what they are doing, and how they are doing it.

- Special note on privacy
  - At times, it will be appropriate to make private rolls to support private actions and skill rolls will need to be made without everyone watching. In those cases, the GM and the player will conduct this in (some form of) secret conversation, such as:
    - Leaving the room for a moment to discuss
    - Use a texting medium, such as iMessage, IMS, etc.

**Attacker** adds the following:

| KEY ABILITY | ABILITY BONUS | Skill/Trait/Power BONUS | MISC BONUS | TOTAL BONUS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | |

Key ability: Ability linked to attack. For example, STR for a warrior or AGI for a rogue

Ability bonus: The actual value of the associated key ability

Skill/Trait/Power bonus: The associated bonus from the appropriate skill/trait or power

Misc. Bonus: Any bonus that may affect your attack: Buff/Aura/Item/etc.

Total Bonus: The sum of all other bonus attack values

**Defense** of the target is determined by the following:

Key ability: Ability linked to defense: For example, AGI for physical attacks, INT for mental attacks, etc.

Ability Bonus: The actual value of the associated key ability

Skill/Trait/Power bonus: The associated bonus from the appropriate skill/trait or power

BASE: All targets have a base.

- Small targets have a base of 75
- Medium targets have a base of 50
- Large targets have a base of 35
- Jumbo targets have a base of 10
- Enormous targets have a base of 5

Shield: Value of the shield being carried by the defender

- Buckler: +5
- Small: +10
- Medium: +15
- Large: +20

Range: Range values for defense:

- Point Blank +0
- Short +10
- Medium +20
- Long +30
- Extreme +50++++ (GM discretion)

Cover: Cover values for defense:

- Line of sight cover (soft) +10
- Up to 50% cover: +25
- Up to 75% cover +50
- More than 75% cover 50++++ (GM discretion)

Misc.: Spell deflection, special circumstances (GM discretion)

**DAMAGE**:

Damage is determined by the same roll as the 'to hit' roll. The more you beat out the target #, the better your damage. Use this chart for initial determination:

Damage Modifier scale:

1-20 = x1

16-45 = x 1.5

46 ++ = x 2

Example:

If you needed to roll a 70 as a target. The following results would alter the amount of damage dealt:

If you rolled a 70-90, you would do stated damage

if you rolled an 91-115, you would do 1.5x stated damage

If you rolled 116 or above, you would do double normal stated damage

**Combat in Sidherun, an example:**

Reeling from his steed collapsing from beneath him, and being thrown down the hill into the brush, Martinuk realized he had no choice but to turn and face the pursuing goblins and their giant wolves. After sliding down the tangled brush covered decline about 20 feet, he came to rest on a fairly flat outcropping, which, much to his dismay dropped another 50 feet behind him, down into some trees. "If they all come at me, I'll have to just try my luck and trust Qaal'el's mercy" he thought as he drew his short sword and dagger combination. Thinking about how he hasn't offered enough to his Goddess in the past month, and wondered if that mercy may come, if he really needed it.

Somewhat to his relief, the two goblins were struggling with their starving wolf mounts as they dove into the fresh meat of Martinuk's borrowed mare, who was too old and slow to outrun them. Growing impatient and lacking the use of their trusted slings due to the tall brush below, the two began their descent, without much in the way of any tactics or strategy. "They want to run right at me, ok, let's send you both back to Gobbligook"

Jay, playing the Woodsman Martinuk and Bobbie-Jean, the GM both use a single 10-sided die to roll initiative, and add that score to their Agility attribute for the characters in the upcoming skirmish.

Jay rolls an 8, which, combined with his agility attribute of 17 gives him a total of 25

BJ has to roll twice for the goblins and rolls 2 and 5

Both goblins have a physical agility of 13, so the initiative for this skirmish is as follows:

Martinuk: 25

Goblin #1: 18

Goblin #2: 15

BJ informs Martinuk that his first round he is at a disadvantage, given his precarious position, and will suffer from being 'surrounded' for the duration of the skirmish, unless he can swap positions with the goblins, and have one or both of them surrounded by the outcrop. Also, he is suffering from being 'downhill', which gives him further disadvantage in this position, unless those change

Given that the Goblins closed too quickly for Martinuk to climb out, he decides he'll use his turn to:

- Make sure he keeps the Goblins in thick brush, so they cannot back up and use their slings (he left his bow on the mare)
- Use his first turn to defend and try position at least one of the goblins on the outcrop, so he can get into a better spot

To do this, BJ asks Jay to roll against any appropriate skill, and Jay decides he wants to use his 'Naturalist' skill, saying that his time in the woods has taught him how to defend himself like this. BJ decides this skill doesn't make sense in this case, and the two decide that Jay will use his base Wisdom to decide how to pull this off. His Wisdom is fairly high (18), and the situation is quite dire, so:

- BJ gives Jay a target number of 90 (difficult)
- Jay rolls a 79 (with his +18 modifier) = 97 – SUCCESS!

Based on the roll above two things happen:

- Jay decides to use skill points at his next level to invest in a skill like 'strategy' or 'tactics' which will help him in the future
- BJ removes his disadvantage from being surrounded, making combat much easier for him

Given that Jay has decided to be defensive for this combat, and is only now suffering from being 'downhill' (as opposed to being both this and 'surrounded'), Jay completes his turn – and Martinuk takes a defensive position to defend himself from the onrushing Goblins

As both goblins descend down the hill, BJ decides that they are rushing downhill in heavy growth and gives them both a charging check, to decide how nimbly the descend the hill.

BJ gives them both a typical roll, as they are both fairly nimble, but this is a steep hill, covered with tight, twisted brambles and they are focused on their prey: A typical roll in this case is given as 60:

Goblin #1 rolls a 51

- 51(roll) + 13(AGI attribute) +20(charging) = 84 -> SUCCESS

Goblin #2 rolls a 04

- Any roll of 01-05 = a fumble -> Oh CRAP!

On any roll set where there is a fumble or critical, the outlier (fumble/crit) is completed first, so:

- Goblin #2 rolls 99 = Very hot on the Fumble heat map – Disastrous result

BJ decides that Goblin #2, in his frothing zeal to get to Martinuk isn't careful enough and gets caught up in the bramble, tripping into Goblin #1, tumbling them both downhill, directly at Martinuk!

Jay asks what his options are, and BJ lets Jay know that, given this is a disastrous result for the goblins, she is going to offer Jay a role play opportunity (great news!) Jay knows if he comes up with something the rest of the group loves, he'll be awarded an extra story point, so he takes a moment and then:

"Martinuk gets into a deep crouch and watches the two buffoons tumble towards him. Realizing the wolves above are going to come for him after they finish the mare, and that he needs to get down into the valley anyway, Martinuk jumps onto the tumbling mass of goblins, grapples with both of them as all 3 tumbles off the outcropping. Martinuk uses his *strong agility, as* they fall, to jump on top of the tangled goblins, and ride them like a surfboard down until the tops of the trees, then jump off them into an appropriate sized tree branch"

The party howls in laughter imagining the scene of the mighty woodsman glistening in his elven chain armor against the afternoon sun. Many 'silver surfer' jokes are made, and they give a nearly universal 'thumbs up', resulting the combat ending with Martinuk jumping safely into mid-sized clump of colossal oak trees deep in the valley below.
