# Character Creation - The Basis for all Characters
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

Sidherun is a game of narration, focused on creating a compelling narrative for players who have invested their time and effort into a character they wish to bring to life. The formative time spent creating the narrative around your character is the genesis of all the actions you'll take going forward. As such, a great deal of time is spent on character story during the creation process.

It's best to have a solid idea ahead of session 0 of what you are looking to bring to life. Some questions that you should have thought about:

- What drives my character?
  - What motivates my character to a life of adventure (could be different than what drives them)
- What areas of my character's history will be most fun to concentrate on?
- Does my character have family?
  - How attached is my character to their family?
- How old is my character?
  - Does my character have life lessons that I can bring into the story?
- Does my character have acquaintances among other players?
  - Do you want to partner on a story with another player?

You should be ready to spend some time writing down the backstory for your character prior to session 0. If necessary, feel free to contact your GM ahead of time to set up your ideas and get feedback, so your session 0 time is best spent.

## Try not to:

- Build your character sheet prior to session 0.
  - Many times, you can collaborate on your character with your GM and have better results
- Miss session 0
  - Many times, this can be one of the most fun sessions you'll attend

## A good character story:

- Is written ahead of time
  - Be willing to make changes, and even provide a copy to your GM ahead of time, if they agree
- Considers the full personality of your character
  - Think about all aspects:
    - Motivation
    - Psychology
    - Family
    - Work/Profession
    - Physical traits
    - Etc.
- Considers what will excite you to play.
  - Don't worry about what classes other players are playing. The GM can make up for party gaps, if you all players are playing a character, they are passionate about
