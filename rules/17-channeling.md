# Channeling
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

Different characters interact with magic differently. As described in the section on Mana, below, this has a lot to do with the mind's ability to comprehend the vastness of the universe and how your mind connects to the weave holding it all together. Some practitioners of magic might believe they can incant a series of phrases and the power of those phrases will enact a change powered by the weave itself. Others believe that their God is the power, so they are merely requesting a change to the universe from an omnipotent being, and, based on their piety, that request is granted.

For some practitioners, however, they believe the manipulation of the weave is done by force of will. For these casters, they believe they must be in a state of concentration to facilitate the actions they expect. The origin of this can still be spiritual or arcane in nature, but the way these casters' minds understand their magic abilities is tied to their will to move the weave to their will.

From a game mechanics perspective:

- Channeling is a style of casting where the practitioner maintains concentration on the activities they are trying to accomplish. This may represent:
  - All the caster's capabilities: meaning, the caster uses this approach all the time or
  - A portion of the caster's usage of magic – where the story indicates a channel is appropriate for the narrative
- When a caster is channeling, the following are in effect:
  - To maintain the activity or effect of the spell that the caster is channeling, all moves, fighting actions and defensive capabilities must include how the caster will deal with maintaining the channel.
    - Example(s):
      - Cara has maintained her focus on the 2nd floor of the building for several hours now. Her low murmured incantation is hardly noticeable to those in the room where she sits, but to Cara, the force she is exerting is quite considerable. She must understand what is going on above the tavern, to help prevent the upcoming insurrection against the Rohnar family in South Shire...
    - Suddenly, the door of her room bursts open and 3 darkly clothed men enter her chamber, faces covered in silk masks. Cara's attention is removed from the spell she is spent days perfecting, and now must consider how she will use what power she has left to defend herself from the intruders...
  - Mana is consumed for the channeling spell at the beginning of each round the caster channels. For many spells, there may be a story accommodation to reduce (or raise) the quantity of mana consumed during channeling
    - Example(s):
      - Josephine, playing Cara, has decided to use her control of the Correspondence Sphere, and cast her gaze upon an area several blocks away from her home, in South Shire. She explains that Cara would like to use this connection to the area to spy on the 2nd floor of a tavern which she believes is connected to a rogue's gallery she is tracking on behalf of the local Duke. The GM indicates that Cara could maintain such a connection using a small, but constant amount of mana over time, which would drain her in about a day, OR Cara could infiltrate the building and set a focus point using a magically infused object that she could channel through. This would cost more up front (to build the foci) but would enable her to maintain the channeling for a far greater amount of time, with lower cost.
  - Channeling magic allows caster some spectacular effects and may even allow effects to last for longer than a caster could normally produce them. However, the act of channeling is typically noticeable by other practitioners of magic, and can result in being discovered
    - Example(s):
      - The 3 initiates were sure that Cara was in the area their Master told them about, but were unsure which building she could be in. Lamor, the gifted one, was able to use his dark arts to 'listen to the wind' and feel where Cara was based on 'lei lines' he tracked to a particular home within the neighborhood that the Master described. When the three men peered into the short, flat thatched-roof cottage in the outskirts of South Shire, they could see nothing, but a single candle lit on a brass holder sitting on a rug next to a thin woman wearing just a long veil wrap who looked like she was sleeping in an unnatural sitting position. "That's the witch" said Lamor, and the three men gathered their weapons before entering Cara's abode.
