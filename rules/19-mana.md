# Mana – The Power Powering Your Power
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

Mana is the name for the measure of how much magical energy your character has access to at any given time. Like hit points, it is a fixed number related to the amount of energy your particular character has. The amount of mana your character has is equal to the number associated with the primary attribute for the type of connection you have to the knit itself.

Example(s):

- Arcane mage archetypes might use Thaumaturgy
- Divine channeling spiritualists might use Enlightenment
- Psychic practitioners might use Intellect
- Warlock archetypes might use Thaumaturgy if the power comes from the arcane realm, or Enlightenment if the Warlock's power comes from an extraordinarily powerful Dimensional being
- Holy Warriors following the Great Light might use Enlightenment
- Other combinations may exist

## Using Mana

Mana is used when casting spells. Your character's mana is tracked on your character sheet. The current total of Mana will exhaust when used. You spend the amount of mana related to the spell you are casting. The process of spending mana is:

- The cost of a spell is related to the level of effect the caster is attempting.
  - The default cost is equal to 'Your Level', as listed on the Spell Matrix chart
  - A player may choose to use a lower-level effect when describing the effect.
- Example(s)
  - Greg wants his character, Blanco, the Mighty Mage, to cast a Mighty Lightning Bolt spell to scare a group of villagers.
  - Blanco, a level 7 Magician Archetype, would roll against the target (bare ground = lvl 1) and needs to roll 95 or lower
  - Greg rolls a 23 (SUCCESS!), and the Mighty Lightning Bolt spell succeeds, however, Greg doesn't want to actually kill the villagers, just scare them. He decides that a [Less] Mighty Lightning Bolt would be more appropriate and wants to only cast 2 levels to burn the ground, but not kill anyone.
  - This scenario would cost Blanco 2 mana

## How Much Mana Do I Have?

Mana is a stable resource. That means a being who has awareness of the way magic weaves together the world will have some amount of power to manipulate that weave. This is tied to the being themselves. The more powerful the user of magic, the more ability they have to manipulate the powerful energies contained therein.

- As a roleplaying device, mana helps the conscience mind understand the vastness of the universe. Mana, and the way it powers a being's ability to use magic, is tied to the translation the character makes between their physical form, and their environment.
  - Imagine waking up one day and seeing the interconnecting fabric of the world as a series of lines, or a cacophony of sounds. Then you realize you have some control over it. How your mind would rationalize that control may depend on your spirituality, your understanding of the arcane, your closeness with religion/nature/etc.
- As a game mechanic, the amount of mana your character possesses is equal to the attribute associated with your spellcasting ability
  - Example(s):
    - If you believe that your ability exists due to a connection with a spirit or God, then you would interact with magic through that understanding. The attribute most strongly associated with this understanding is Enlightenment
    - If you believe that your ability to manipulate the knit of the universe exists due to your understanding of magic itself, then you would interact with these energies through that understanding. As such, you would use the Thaumaturgy attribute
  - Which attribute you use for your magic abilities is determined during the character creation phase, typically during session 0.
