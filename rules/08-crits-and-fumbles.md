## Critical Rolls and their Use in Sidherun

### In Combat:

**"CRITS"**

A '*critical hit*' is determined anytime a player rolls a natural % roll of 96-00. When this happens, the following rules are in effect:

- Roll the % dice again. If you rolled 96-00, continue rolling until you don't.
  - ***NOTE*** You continue to roll crit multipliers until you roll <96.

### Effects of a crit - Damage - MELEE:

If you roll a natural crit, you automatically add your **STRENGTH** value to the damage done, **for each Additional Roll** regardless of the final number rolled

- Example(s):
  - Martinuk attempts to strike the stubborn Orc Marauder he has been fighting for far too long, as both are now close to exhausted, Martinuk knows he needs to end this before something else wanders along the forest path:
    - Jay, playing Martinuk rolls to strike the Orc with his combined short sword and fighting knife, rolling a 97, a CRIT!
      - Jay, excited, picks up his dice and rolls again, this time rolling a 99, a DOUBLE CRIT!
      - Shaking the dice again, the third roll yields Jay a 46, ending his Crit rolls with a great number!
    - The total of Martinuk's strike on the Orc is:
      - 78 –> 18(AGI)+30(Dual Wield Skill) +20(fighting from above) +10(Martinuk has an affinity for fighting orcs) = 78
      - The orc has a defense of 70 -> 50(Base)+15(AGI)+5(Small Buckler)
      - Jay's roll: 244 (2 extra rolls)
        - TOTAL DAMAGE:
          - Fighting Knife = 4x2(roll>45) =8
          - Short Sword = 6x2(roll>45) =12
          - 2xcrit rolls = 30 (Martinuk has a strength of 15)
          - = 50 points!
      - With a final gut-wrenching howl, the Orc in front of Martinuk drops to his knees and then to his face, bleeding out from multiple wounds, including the two deep gouges from the final blow!

### ADDITIONAL MODIFIERS - DAMAGE

On occasion, your rolls will overcome the target by more than a factor of 2. In this case, the damage multiplier will scale with your roll.

- Example(s):
  - Kleg the Barbarian is angry. He's looking at the puny man in front of him and wondering why he's in Kleg's seat. Kleg decides to smash puny man's face onto the table and take his seat back.
  - Bobby, playing Kleg, is going to use a grapple slam attack to try and incapacitate the poor drunk sailor whose only transgression against humanity is being drunk in a seat that Kleg thinks is his. Bobby rolls to grab and slam the sailor:
    - Bobby's bonuses: 19(STR)+10(Grapple Modifier for Kleg) +10(Attack from behind) = 39
    - Sailor's defense: 50(Base)-25(almost passed out)-15(unsuspecting) = 10
    - Bobby rolls a 24 (normally a miss), but with the +39 bonus, this gives Kleg a total of 63, which is:
      - >45 points more than target *and*
      - 3x the roll needed
    - Kleg's normal grapple-slam attack does 8 points of damage (special ability), but in this case, Kleg does 24 (3x normal damage), doing more than 50% of the sailor's hit points in a single blow, and knocking him through the table that Kleg wanted to sit at. Now Kleg is even angrier at the little puny man who broke Kleg's table!

## Fumbles - Sometimes things just don't work out

At times, the best laid plans of your character just won't agree with fate. This occurs when, much to the chagrin of players, the dice do not cooperate, and you are left with a roll result of 01-05. When this happens, this is considered a 'fumble' and your plans have come to a poor end.

### Fumble Mechanics:

When, at any time in the game, a player rolls 01-05 on any dice roll, that roll is considered a fumble. The player rolls a 2nd time, and the GM considers the appropriate Heat chart (example here). There are many different HEAT charts the GM uses (or may choose to use), depending on the scenario, their whim, etc. In the end, the 2nd roll performed by the player determines the basis for what happens next. The higher the roll, the more difficult the result for the player (and certainly the player's party)

- Example(s):
  - Martinuk, having defeated the two orcs who harassed him as he made his way across the valley approached the small patrol of King's Town's finest – the Pompadiers – encamped along the main road South to Quin-Tiel. Raising his right arm to his chest in salute, the former Sergeant in Duke Rohnar's army decided he'd try and act as though he was familiar with the military, but not a former member, so the patrol would not ask him too many questions. The patrol was quite wary of the passing traveler but invited him to sit with them while they had a light trail meal.

Seated next to the largest member of the patrol, Martinuk offered the large man a small vial of whiskey from a pouch. Passing it with his left hand across his body, Martinuk forgot about the tattoo of his unit emblazoned across his forearm. Martinuk tried rolling his wrist to ensure the patrol member didn't see the markings.

Jay, playing Martinuk asked the GM to have a roll against his 'sleight of hand' (a Skill Martinuk has as a former street urchin).

- Jay rolls a 03 (Oh Crap, a Fumble)

The GM instructs Jay to roll on the Fumble heat chart. Jay rolls a 71, very unlucky indeed!

The GM decides that this particular patrol had been in a recent bar brawl with Martinuk's former unit, and their beloved Sergeant was put into jail for a fortnight, then the unit was put on patrol for an entire Moon cycle.

The large soldier notices the markings on your arm, takes the whiskey and throws it in the fire. Standing up, the man rolls his sleeves up and says, 'Oh, you fucks want some more, do ye'?

### Fumble(s) in Combat:

In Combat, a fumble can be catastrophic, up to and including a character's death, depending on the

- Thing the character is trying to do
- The narrative at that time in the game
- The 'obviousness' of the danger involved

Example(s)

- After listening to a wizard's telling of the tale of how a singular potion caused the innards of a creature to come spilling out its belly, Kleg the Barbarian decides he wants to try the potion himself, to prove how strong he is. The wizard offers Kleg another word of caution, explaining that the last time he saw a person drink from this vial, they met a terrible, painful end. Kleg decides he's too strong for this and wants to drink the potion.

Browga, the most senior member of the party says, "Are ye daft, you damn fool? If this cursed vial spills your innards, ain't nothin we can do for ye. You'll die screaming in the middle of this here courtyard as I ain't got nothing to heal stupid, and our priest is off doing wutever priests do. You'll die, you moron!"

Kleg, never one for reason, thinks there's been enough talking, taking the glass vial from the wizard's hand and downs it before anyone can speak.

Within a few moments, he begins to feel a strong burning sensation in the midsection.

The GM asks Bobbie, who is playing Kleg to roll a poison check, using only his constitution as a modifier, as Kleg has just swallowed a type of fast-acting acid. The best thing that can happen here is that Kleg may survive long enough to get someone to heal him.

Kleg has a constitution of 20 (that's great) and asks for the target. The GM tells him 90 (very hard, but appropriate considering the situation)

Bobbie rolls a 03, Oh NO, a FUMBLE!

Bobbie then has to roll another set of dice and decide how poorly things are going to be for Kleg. Bobbie rolls an 80. This might be the end for Kleg. Perhaps a Story Point may save him?

- In this situation (we'll cover Story Points later), it would be entirely appropriate for the GM to kill off Kleg, based on the situation (drinking acid), the roll (03), and the narrative (dumb barbarian does dumb barbarian things)
