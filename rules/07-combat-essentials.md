# Combat in Sidherun – Essential Rules
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

## Combat in Sidherun – Essential Rules:

At its most basic, Sidherun combat is a specialized type of skill usage.

- Combat, like the rest of Sidherun is narrative-driven, meaning, rules can be discarded by the GM, as needed, to drive the story
- Combat, as a Skill has some specific considerations:
  - There is typically an attacker and a defender (or many defenders)
  - All combat scenarios occur 'at the same time', with the initiative roll used to infer the slight differentiation speed brings to a fight
  - A combatant who uses multiple weapons, has multiple attacks, etc., still describes their story for the round and **all of their combat happens as a result of the story and a single roll**
    - Example(s):
      - Browga Hammersmith, the great Dwarven Guardsman, decides to use both of his attacks with his mighty maul on the Stone Giant in front of him. Ed, playing Browga, describes a mighty twirling swing that involves a hammer-throw-like motion, resulting in maximum damage to the Giant
        - BJ the GM gives Ed a target of 75 to hit the Giant (fairly easy, given Browga's level) the results:
          - 56(roll)+50(Browga's Skill with Maul) +25(Bonus hitting Lg Creatures) = 131 (SUCCESS)
          - Browga calculates damage for **BOTH** Maul strikes at once.
  - When targeting another character, the player's target is the target character's *Defensive Rating (see below)*

## Attacker Considerations:

When deciding to attack, a player can typically expect to consider the following:

- Key Attribute: Ability linked to attack. For example, STR for a warrior or AGI for a rogue
- Ability bonus: The actual value of the associated key ability, along with any special modifiers from Attribute
- Skill/Trait/Power bonus: The associated bonus from the appropriate skill/trait or power
- Misc. Bonus: Any bonus that may affect your attack: Buff/Aura/Item/etc.
- Total Bonus: The sum of all other bonus attack values

## Defender Considerations:

**Defense** of a target is based on many factors, such as those below:

- Base: Each character has a certain Base Defense, which takes into account the difficulty in making a standard attack against a creature of that size
- Key Attribute: Ability linked to defense: For example, AGI for physical attacks, INT for mental attacks, etc.
- Attribute Bonus: The actual value of the associated key ability, along with any special modifiers from Attribute
- Skill/Trait/Power bonus: The associated bonus from the appropriate skill/trait or power
- Shield: A character using a shield gains a bonus to defense
- Misc. Bonus: Any bonus that may affect your attack: Buff/Aura/Item/etc.
- Total Bonus: The sum of all other bonus defensive values

**BASE**: All targets have a base.

- Small targets have a base of 75
- Medium targets have a base of 50
- Large targets have a base of 35
- Jumbo targets have a base of 10
- Enormous targets have a base of 5

**Key Attribute**: The Name of the associated attribute

**Ability Bonus**: The Attribute Value associated with that type of Defense

- If you list 'Agility' as your Key Attribute for 'Typical' Defense, then the Agility Attribute Value would go into this column

**Skill/Trait/Power Bonus**: Add the bonus associated with your unique character for this Defense

- Example(s): If you had a Power that gave you skin as tough as bark, you may be granted an associated bonus of 5 to your Typical Defense

**Shield**: Value of the shield being carried by the defender

- Buckler: +5
- Small: +10
- Medium: +15
- Large: +20

**MISC:** Your character may have other Bonuses, such as:

- Something granted by a magic item
- A Curse has been placed on you
- Spell deflection, special circumstances (GM discretion)

## Other Situational Modifiers

**Range**: Range values for defense:

- Point Blank +0
- Short +10
- Medium +20
- Long +30
- Extreme +50++++ (GM discretion)

**Cover**: Cover values for defense:

- Line of sight cover (soft) +10
- Up to 50% cover: +25
- Up to 75% cover +50
- More than 75% cover 50++++ (GM discretion)

### Generating Damage

Damage is determined by the **same roll** as the 'to hit' roll. The more you beat out the target #, the better your damage. Use this chart for initial determination:

Damage Modifier scale:

- 1-20 = x1
- 16-45 = x 1.5
- 46 ++ = x 2

Example(s):

- Martinuk, in a fight for his life, has incapacitated one orc and the 2nd is bleeding badly. As he struggles to press his advantage, he strikes at his foe once again.

Martinuk, fighting with a short sword and a fighting knife leapt at the orc, striking at his chest.

- Jay, Martinuk's player is trying to strike an orc he's with two weapons.
  - Martinuk has an attack bonus of 78 –> 18(AGI)+30(Dual Wield Skill) +20(fighting from above) +10(Martinuk has an affinity for fighting orcs) = 78
  - The orc has a defense of 70 -> 50(Base)+15(AGI)+5(Small Buckler)
  - Martinuk rolls 90+78 (attack)=168 (SUCCESS)
  - 168-70 = 98, which means that Martinuk does double the normal damage of his dual attacks!

> ✅ **Ruling (Ed, 2026-07-11):** the attribute and the weapon skill do **not** stack on an attack roll — a skilled character uses the weapon-skill value, an unskilled one uses the governing attribute, never both. Situational and misc bonuses (fighting from above, affinities, buffs, items) still add on top. This example's "18(AGI)+30(Dual Wield Skill)" summing is superseded on that point: under the ruling, Martinuk's attack bonus is 30(Dual Wield) + 20 + 10 = **60**. See FIDELITY-NOTES §1.13 (resolved).

- Simple examples:
  - If you needed to roll a 70 as a target. The following results would alter the amount of damage dealt:
    - If you rolled a 70-85, you would do normal stated damage
    - if you rolled an 86-115, you would do 1.5 stated damage
    - If you rolled 116 or above, you would do double normal stated damage
