# Story Points – Driving the Narrative forward
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

Sometimes a party of player characters does everything right. They have practiced excellent role playing, have gone above and beyond to advance the story, and have taken the right strategic approach to defeat the big bad guy (BBG). Sometimes all things go right, only to find they can go horribly wrong due to a particularly poor dice roll.

***Story Points*** are a way to help the narrative (and the character) continue onward in such cases.

- Each player starts out the game, regardless of level with 2 *Story Points*
- A GM may reward more story points at any time, for whatever reason. Common examples of activities that receive story point awards:
  - A new level
  - An amazing, story-moving action by a character
  - A selfless action resulting in a great narrative

## How to use Story Points:

- ***Story Points*** can be used for 2 different purposes:
  - *For 1 * **Story Point, ** *you can:*
    - Re-roll **any roll, even when rules do not normally allow it**
    - Add a second roll to a first roll, adding the two rolls together
      - Ex: Browga has attempted to swing his mighty maul at the small Quickling for several combat rounds and has not been able to connect with anything. The player announces they will use a *Story Point to add an additional roll to the first:*
        - Browga's first roll 56
        - Browga's second roll (received by using a *Story Point) *71
          - New total = 127, which allows him to roll again (as any roll total over a 95 is a crit) - see RESOLVING COMBAT for more information
  - You can use **two ** story points to:
    - Obtain an automatic 100 on a roll, allowing the player to roll again – see CRITICAL and FUMBLE ROLLS for more information in critical rolls
      - For this use, you must declare you are using your two-Story *Points * **PRIOR to rolling**
    - Cause another character to roll an automatic 01, forcing that character to roll again – see CRITICAL and FUMBLE ROLLS for more information on fumbles
