# Resolving Combat - When is the hill *ACTUALLY Taken?*
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

As described in the section "Order of Operations", combat occurs in turns, divided by rounds which permit each character from taking their actions. At the end of each turn of combat, the characters involved with the conflict will have many options before them.

- Fight – Continue to fight onward, as they believe they have the ability to win (or some other goal)
- Flight – Combatants can decide they've had enough and decide to move away from their enemy combatants, either voluntarily or involuntarily
- Conflict ends with one side or the other no longer able to fight
- Some other outcomes:
  - This can be anything from, strategic retreat, to capture all the enemy, to whatever you can imagine.

## Some rules to help

Typically, the GM will know the motivation for the non-player characters involved in a combat and have well-plotted narratives as to 'why' they do what they are doing. Sometimes, however, random occurrences can change the plan, and to account for that, optional combat turn ending rules exist. These (most often) happen during more random-type encounters where the narrative is more like, 'the party runs into *something*' as opposed to a dedicated plot point. However, these optional rules may apply at any time the GM needs them:

## Tenacity Check

- At the end of any full round, the GM can roll a 'tenacity check' for each non-player character to determine if that character sticks around, or breaks ranks
  - A 'Tenacity Check' is based on the scenario and is typically based on a core attribute such as Wisdom. If the check is failed, that non-player character may decide they've had enough of the conflict and change their behavior. This change will be noted by the GM and that character may attempt to withdraw, fight harder, throw more destructive spells, etc., depending on the situation
  - Other uses for 'Tenacity Checks'
    - A non-player character takes >50% of their total hit points in a single blow
    - A core character or ally is killed during a conflict, resulting in the rest of that group to reconsider the plan
    - Anytime a non-player character has a significant event occur which causes them to rethink their plan
      - A spell such as confusion or fear may force a Tenacity Check as well

## Withdrawing from Combat

- When a non-player character withdraws, flees, or takes any action to withdraw from a combat where they are engaged, the GM will update the scenario with this information (as appropriate) and the players involved may be given some sort of out-of-turn action as a result
  - Example(s):
    - Martinuk and Kleg are fighting a stubborn Stone Troll, who has taken quite a bit of damage from the two mighty warriors and is now reconsidering his lot in life. At the end of a combat round, the GM decides the Stone Troll has had enough and will attempt to flee.
      - The GM gives both Kleg and Martinuk an out-of-combat attack on the creature as it turns to run, and further attempts to harass the creature if they so choose, especially given the size and speed difference (Kleg and Martinuk are much faster and more nimble)
        - These attacks are given at the back of the Stone Troll, resulting in a better chance to hit the creature, if they decide to do that
