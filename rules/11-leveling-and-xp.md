Characters gain XP per gaming session. The GM decides how to distribute XP based on player and character actions. As the story moves along, the XP awarded will increase in importance, thus the gradient can be easily overcome by the players advancing the story as it becomes more difficult/suspenseful. A good night at level 1 may yield 250 XP, while a good night at level 5 may yield 4000.

Characters gain XP by player vote per gaming session: The players, at the conclusion of each gaming session may vote for 1 player (NOT THEMSELVES) to receive a bonus XP value (typically 10% of a level) by using the following criteria:

- Best Role Playing
- Most important for story
- Best story driver

*Example(s):*

- Four players of level 2 are awarded 250 XP for a game night where the story advanced as planned/better than plan. One player, chosen by the team, would advance an extra 200.
- Four players of level 5 are awarded 4000 XP for a game night where the story advanced as planned/better than plan. One player, chosen by the team, would advance an extra 600.
