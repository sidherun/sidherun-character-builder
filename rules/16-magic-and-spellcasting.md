## Magic as a plot point

Magic in Sidherun is controlled (as much as Magic is ever controlled) by the 'Awakened' - a group attuned to the energies knitted, or woven, into the universe. This knit, or pattern, is an invisible energy force that flows through and around all beings in the universe. When a student, acolyte, warlock, or any other practitioner of the arts casts a spell, controls a dark creature, communes with a spirit or God, or becomes one with the universe, their own spirit interweaves with that energy and gives those attuned the ability to generate, dampen or manipulate that energy.

The cost to use this type of energy differs by the character, based on their story. Some casters use books and magic gleaned from years of study in dusty old libraries, others have a trust in their god such they know they will have what they need, when they need it (as long as they remain faithful). For each type of caster, there are specific rules that define how they do what they do. As the magic 'system' in Sidherun is based on an attunement to a greater energy, with varied ways to attune, the GM and player defined the rules for that particular character, based on the story told at the beginning.

There are many examples we will walk through, based on diverse types of practitioners that have lived their lives in the Druidshome, Southern Shores, Obilish, the Kaelorun Range, and many of the other places around Sidherun.

From a base 'system' perspective, see the section(s) 'Spell System' and 'Powers' for more information and details. Remember, though, there are more ways to manipulate the knit of the universe than are currently (or have ever been) known, and it's up to each character to move towards discovery via curiosity, creativity, and wonder.

## Magic – The Fickle Mistress

Magic in Sidherun relies on incalculable variations of energy, availability, connection, location, faith, purpose, etc. As such, it's important to remember that magic in the world of Sidherun comes with a bit of randomness, depending on what a character is trying to do, when they try to do it, and if the target of such magic is willing or able to be impacted.

Each target has some sort of connection to the knit and their connection might be strong and fluid, or act like a resister in a circuit. In game terms, this is represented by their Spell Defense, Psychic Defense, or in some cases one of the other Defenses represented in the table below:

The target of your magic spell, effect, etc., may have knowledge, experience, or resistance to the magical arts a character deploys and each of these are treated as modifier to the roll, just as if you were trying any other skill.

## Some notes:

- Casting a spell against a lesser opponent yields a higher chance to impact the target, as well as an increased opportunity to do more damage, especially when the target is significantly lower
- The spellcaster *ALWAYS has a chance to fail a spell*, no matter the caster level, target level or gap between the two (magic *is a fickle mistress*)

## Spells in Sidherun

As described in the section, 'How does Magic Work', the magic system (and all spell casting) in Sidherun exists to drive the narrative, provide interesting plot opportunities, and alter the power dynamic between characters. It's purposefully *NOT* designed to be 100% *FAIR.* If magic existed in the real world, the rules which govern it would apply, and the practitioners would do what they could to differentiate themselves via power, skill, or any other method, to become more than the others. Such is the story in Sidherun.

## Basic Spellcasting in Sidherun

Spellcasting in Sidherun comes in several forms, but all have the same basic components:

- Some way to control the knit of the universe
- Some way for the target to resist the caster's abilities
- Some way to limit how much control the caster has

- A character describes to the GM what they wish to do
- The GM lets the player who wishes their character to cast a spell know what, if any, modifications (difficulty) is assigned to the spell (see general rules below the Spell Matrix table
- If the character targeted is a player character, they may have a Spell Defense attribute. If so, this modifier is noted in the next
- The player reviews the Spell Matrix table, comparing their level with the target level. The number revealed is the "Spell Target"
- Modify the Spell Target if the GM has assigned a modifier, or if the player character has a Spell/Psychic Defense, as appropriate
- The player needs to roll **below** the Spell Target number for the spell to take effect

Example(s):

- Ingrid, the level 5 Druid, wants to cast a spell targeting a Dark Initiate who has been using their haunting magic to *spook* and *confuse* members of Ingrid's team with a silencing spell. Drawing on her knowledge of the natural world, she wants to use the surrounding caverns to her advantage. Her spell will pull on the granite dust laying on the cave walls to surround the Dark Initiate's head with choking dust, rendering her foe unable to speak (or continue to cast spells)
- Bonnie, playing Ingrid, explains her plan to BJ, the GM. BJ believes the Dark Initiate (level 4) would be at a serious disadvantage if silenced, and loves the narrative of, 'how' Ingrid would use the natural world to perform this feat. She does not assign a modifier.
- Bonnie reviews the Spell Matrix Table, and using the dynamic properties, adds her relevant Attribute (in this case, it's Enlightenment, 15), which updates the chart. (See screenshot below). As Ingrid is a level 5 character, and her target is a level 4, she has an adjusted Spell Target of 75
- Ingrid rolls two ten-sided dice and 53 (SUCCESS!). Her spell works and Bonnie describes the effect to the group
