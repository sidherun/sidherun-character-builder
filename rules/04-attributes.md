# Attributes in Sidherun
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

**Attributes** (also called Ability Scores) reflect the various capabilities of your character, at a base level, and are generated during a character creation session with the player and GM. The player describes the background of the character (see "Character Story") to the GM and the GM helps place the character's attributes, as appropriate. All the players in Sidherun are offered the option to play characters of exceptional mettle, as they represent the finest individuals of their tribe/village/town/city/etc. As such, during character creation, the goal would be to create a character with an average of '15' across the top 10 attributes. Comeliness and Fame are not included in the calculations.

## Attribute Definitions:

### Core Attributes:

**Strength**: Represents the *Physical strength* of the character – the ability to 'pick things up and put them down'

- Example(s):
  - Lifting/Holding heavy objects
  - Moving heavy objects across a surface
  - Used to help boost physical damage during combat

**Physical Agility:** A measurement of how a character can move their *entire body as one*.

- Example(s):
  - Sneaking around in the dark
  - Moving carefully through large traps
  - Dodging a blow
  - Etc.

**Manual Dexterity**: A measurement of the character's *Hand - Eye coordination*

- Example(s):
  - Aiming a bow
  - Intricate finger motions involved in casting a spell
  - Picking someone's pocket
  - Etc.

**Endurance:** A measurement of the character's *Fitness level*

- *Example(s):*
  - Running Long Distances
  - Marching over difficult terrain
  - Manipulating difficult magic
  - Mental or emotional drain
  - Etc.

**Constitution:** The character's ability to take *Physical Damage*

- Example(s):
  - How many Hit Points your character has before they are destroyed
  - Physical Damage Avoidance via luck or saving throws
  - Etc.

**Intellect:** Your character's raw intellectual horsepower as measured by *Knowledge and Retention*

- Example(s):
  - "Can I remember what happened 'n' number of days/years ago?"
  - If I'm a caster of magic from books, how much can I remember?
  - "Do I remember you?"
  - Etc.

**Wisdom:** Your character's ability to *Understand and Create Linkages across disciplines*

- Example(s):
  - "Can I rationalize this enemy's intent based on what I have experienced thus far?"
  - Used to link different schools of thought together
    - Blessed Lightning Bolt

**Thaumaturgy:** Represents the character's magical abilities as they pertain to the *Arcane and Supernatural*

- Example(s):
  - Thaumaturgy drives arcane-magic usage as an attribute
  - Thaumaturgy is used in contested result roles where two arcane users attempt to over channel each other

**Enlightenment:** Represents a character's *Attunement with the Gods and/or Spirituality*

- Example(s):
  - Enlightenment drives divine-magic usage as an attribute
  - Enlightenment is used in contested result roles where two divine spell casters attempt to over channel each other

**Charisma:** Represents the Personality of your character as a score

- Example(s):
  - Charm, wit, persuasion
  - Charisma is used when a roll is more appropriate for a game condition that true role playing:
    - "I need to persuade the guard to do something he doesn't want to do"
  - Charisma is used in contested result roles when two characters need to settle an argument

## Social Attributes

Social Attributes play a specific role in Sidherun. While they do not fall into the 'Core Attributes' section and are considered outside the initial Attribute allotment, they allow the players to really flesh out the impact the characters will have within a society.

**Comeliness:** Physical Attraction to your own race

- Example(s):
  - Used in combination with Charisma to charm a non-player character
  - Used in coordination with various spell-types to enhance outcomes
  - Can make it more difficult to, 'blend in'

**Fame:** How likely you are to be known by your own race/group/region

- Example(s):
  - Used when entering a new area, to determine reception
  - Used in combination with various other attributes to enhance a charm-based encounter
  - Used to increase (or decrease) luck points when in an affected area
