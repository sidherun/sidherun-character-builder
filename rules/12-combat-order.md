# Combat Order
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

When characters in Sidherun come into conflict, use the following order of operations to determine what happens, and when:

- Game Master Describes scene
  - All conflict areas have a scene, which is described by the GM. As part of the scene description, the GM may ask the players specific questions such as:
    - Where is your character on this map/chart/etc.
    - Are you doing anything specific now?
  - The point of scene setting is to ensure the drama and action to come starts from a common understanding of what is seen/felt in the area where the characters begin
- Conflict begins – All parties determine order
  - Each player rolls a single 10-sided die to determine order of operation, and adds any modifiers, such as:
    - Agility Attribute – most common
    - Personal Modifier – common for certain types of archetypes in Sidherun
    - Situational Modifier – uncommon, but certainly occurs
  - The combined initiative order number (your ten-sided roll plus any modifiers) are placed in order, top to bottom
  - The character with the highest combined score acts first
  - Secondary characters (pets, familiars, bodyguards, etc., have their own initiative order and should be rolled separately
- From the highest initiative order number, the first character acts
  - This action is called a 'round' (see Combat and Movement section for details on movement)
  - All actions within that 'turn' are taken by the character during their round
    - This means, movement, adjustments, combat, spell casting, etc., with appropriate exceptions as noted in skills/spells etc.)
    - All actions and impact are resolved (spells complete/channeled, movement completed, damage applied, etc.)
  - *Note on 'Holding'*
    - A character may decide they are not yet ready to take an action when it is their turn. If that happens, they may hold and act at any initiative after their own.
      - Example(s)
        - Ed, the GM, collects the initiative scores from all 5 players and 3 Ogres and lists them in a table:

| Martinuk | 27 | Ingrid | 16 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feisty | 21 | Ogre #2 | 15 |
| Ogre #1 | 19 | Ogre #3 | 12 |
| Badger | 18 | Browga | 11 |

- After assembling the list, Ed begins to count down from the top number, 27. Jay, playing Martinuk, wants to perform a counter on Ogre #1, who he is confronting alone in a corner, decides he wants to hold his turn until *right after* Ogre #1.
  - Ed asks Neil, who is playing Feisty the Rogue to take his action
  - After Neil's action is complete, Ogre #1 takes (yet another) mighty swing against Martinuk, and Jay announces he'd like to use a special ability, called parry-thrust, which Martinuk has built over his time fighting in close quarters. This will allow Martinuk to use the Ogres power against himself.
    - For this round, Martinuk would take his action *right after* the Ogre (19.1), and then return, as normal in the next round to his place in the initiative (27)
- *Note on 'Ties'*
  - When two characters have the same combined initiative score, the scores remain, and the character with the higher *base attribute* goes first in that slot. The initiative order is 'clumped' and separated by a decimal point on whatever table is being used to track initiative
    - Example(s)
      - In round two of the above combat, Browga, Ogre #2 and Ogre #3 all have a combined Initiative of 14. These 3 combatants would be ordered by the AGI Attribute:

| Ogre #3 | 14 |
|---|---|
| Browga | 14.1 |
| Ogre #2 | 14.2 |

- If 2 or more characters have the same combined initiative score, and the same AGI, then the players involved roll another single ten-sided die. This second roll determines order within the original initiative spot
  - Example(s)
    - In round 3 of the above combat, Ingrid, and Ogre #1 both have the same roll (15) and the same AGI attribute (10).
    - The GM and Bonnie, who plays Ingrid, both roll a new 10-sided die.
      - Bonnie rolls 9
      - GM rolls 3
    - In this example, both combatants would take their actions in the 15 slots, and Ingrid would go first:

| Ingrid | 15 |
|---|---|
| Ogre #1 | 15.1 |

- Each character (both player and non-player) takes their actions for this round
- Once each player has taken their actions, resolve any conditions that remain unresolved
  - Example(s)
    - If a character has been reduced to < zero hit points, they will continue to lose 1 per round until their soul leaves their physical body and they die.
- The turn completes when all characters have had their action turn and all unresolved conditions are worked
