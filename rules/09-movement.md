# Movement and Time – General Rules
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

Movement in general game terms is defined by the story pace. There will be times when movement feels slow and cumbersome, and other times when the time of the game will pass quickly, as the party of adventurers will overcome large swaths of terrain to achieve a goal or move to the next plot line. There are some exceptions around movement and time that need to be called out:

## Combat

Timing within combat helps to define the outcome of a fight. As such, there are some important things to remember around timing within combat rounds:

- *Combat rounds: * Combat is measured in 10 second 'rounds'
  - Each round consists of each character getting 1 to *n * actions during their turn
  - All of a character's actions occur at the same time for purposes of game mechanics
    - Example(s):
      - Browga Hammersmith, the Mountain Dwarf Guardsman is a 5th level combat veteran. As he has achieved this status, he has tuned his fighting style to be able to conduct two swings with his mighty maul in each round. *BOTH swings (actions) occur during Browga's turn within a combat round*
  - Each character has a defined amount of distance they may travel, within a combat round. This distance is defined during character creation and is based on the base movement table (below
  - *SPECIAL NOTES:*
    - *Sidherun is story-driven first, which means there will be times when (for dramatic effect or other reasons) a GM may decide to break up actions – This is done during play and always intended to better drive the story*
    - **Channel***ing can occur over several rounds, which gives the character doing the channeling special rules as to when actions can be taken.*

The higher your AGI, the further you can move in a round. The speeds listed here are the MAX you can move at a run in a 10 second movement phase.

## Flight/Special Move Notes:

- **STRONG: ** Faster speed allows a character to make 1 sharp turn while on the ground
- **HEROIC: ** Faster speed allows a character to make 2 sharp turns while on the ground. *ALSO:*
  - Allows fliers to fly at 2x the speed listed above
- **NOBLE: ** Faster speed allows a character to make 2 sharp turns while running. *ALSO:*
  - Running does not exclude a normal action, ex: You can Run full speed and take a (story appropriate) normal action
  - Allows fliers to fly at 3x the speed listed above
- **ENHANCED: ** Faster speed allows a character to make unlimited sharp turns while running. *ALSO: *
  - Running does not exclude a normal action, ex: You can Run full speed and take a (story appropriate) normal action
  - Allows fliers to fly at 4x the speed listed above
  - All attacks on a character operating at **ENHANCED ** speed include the movement attribute as a defensive modifier.
    - Ex.: Browga attempts to strike a quickling operating at 24 speed, who is running circles around the powerful Mountain Dwarf. Browga must also include the speed modifier in his attack target. (See RESOLVING COMBAT section for more details)
- **AUGMENTED: ** Faster speed allows a character to make unlimited sharp turns while running. *ALSO:*
  - Running does not exclude a normal action, ex: You can Run full speed and take a (story appropriate) normal action
  - Allows fliers to fly at 5x the speed listed above
  - All attacks on a character operating at **AUGMENTED ** speed include *DOUBLE * the movement attribute as a defensive modifier.
    - Ex.: Browga attempts to strike a wind spirit operating at 28 speed, who is flying about the powerful Mountain Dwarf. Browga must also include double (56) the speed modifier in his attack target. (See RESOLVING COMBAT section for more details)
- **CELESTIAL: ** Speed beyond mortal understanding. Special rules pertain to each being who has achieved this level of speed, which your GM will explain, as appropriate.

- Armor impacts the character's ability to move due to its fit, weight, etc.

- "Shift Movement Left":
  - When you wear armor, it slows you down. From a game mechanics perspective, the reduction impacts your physical Agility score. The column 'Shift Movement Left' refers to the 'Descriptor' row on the Movement chart at the top of this page.
  - When you have your Descriptor shifted left, you perform these steps:
    - Look at your character's current Agility Score, note the Descriptor row name
    - Count the number of Descriptor columns from where you start to the new armor. Your ADJUSTED AGI is the highest number in that category
  - Example(s)
    - Martinuk currently wears padded armor, which does not impede his movement. As someone who needs to move quickly and silently, he really values the fact that his armor doesn't slow him down. After several battles, however, Martinuk is savagely beaten and bruised and is starting to think about adding some more stout armor, to avoid the pain caused by these adventures.

Jay, playing Martinuk, reviews the Armor Movement table and thinks about impact of adding leather armor to Martinuk:

- Martinuk would have some reduced damage (4-6 pts per shot, which would greatly reduce healing needed on him)
- He'd also see his movement reduced from the additional armor.
  - His physical agility would be reduced from 'Strong'(17) to 'Capable'(15)
    - This happens because the leather armor moves you 1 Descriptor left
    - This would reduce his initiative by 2 points every turn (from 17 to 15, as Martinuk's new Adjusted AGI would be 15, while wearing armor)
