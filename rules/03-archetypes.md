# Archetypes in Sidherun
<!-- source: Sidherun PHB 2_8_2026.docx -->

Archetypes, or classes, in Sidherun mirror common professions found in your favorite fantasy, sci-fi, or other fiction books. The purpose of an archetype is to help build healthy guardrails around your character, and to help you understand where to take (and not take) the progression of your character over time. Think of an archetype as a starter story that you can build on over time.

For many players, envisioning the wizard, robed in long, flowing mage robes, with a clever hat and a key tool that acts as a focus for their magic (such as a walking stick or wand) conjures many different memories, emotions, and opportunities for narrative and creativity. While there is a story that exists where this wizard suddenly pulls a 40-pound maul from the nether and crushes someone's skull, it is a story that exists for a particular narrative. Is this your narrative? Maybe, or maybe not.

Using an archetype to guide the creation of your character can go a long way to help you envision their strengths, weaknesses, and biggest areas to impact the story. Having said this, archetypes can also introduce boring tropes that have been played out for some players. There is a balance that should be maintained, and it is up to each player to understand where their interests and limits lie.

The preliminary work around character creation consists of (typically) a pre-session-0 chat, where the player runs their idea by the GM, and it is the GM's mission to help the player develop and document something which will excite the player, engage the GM, and drive a strong narrative. Sometimes, fantasy tropes are a lot of fun to play, other times, the GM may help the player delve deeper into the drives, passions, or motivations of a character to tweak a trope into something truly unique.

To spur thoughts and ideas, here is a listing of some common archetypes, to use as a basis for your own character creation. Stuck? Ask your group for ideas, ask your GM to help flesh something out, or pull a favorite character from a story as inspiration. Remember, the goal here is to drive compelling narratives, have a wonderful time, and walk away with amazing stories you and your friends will share for a lifetime!
