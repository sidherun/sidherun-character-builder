# The Sidhe'dhel — Elvenkind

*What any elf character knows, and what anyone raised among elves would have heard.*

## The two kindreds

Elvenkind as a whole are the **Sidhe'dhel** ("spirit-kin"). Humans say "elves," or use Quin'dhel loosely for all of them. There are two kindreds:

- The **Quin'dhel** ("kin of the Grove") are the high elves. They reckon themselves by lineage — which line you descend from matters more than where you live. Their heartwood is **Qu'Droia**, the Sylvan Wood of the central interior.
- The **Gla'mdroi** ("folk of the Wild-Hold") are the wild elves. They reckon themselves by the hold they belong to. Their seat is **Gla'mDroia** in the western foothills of the Kaelorun Range.

The kindreds share one language, one liturgy, and one duty. They differ in day-to-day speech, in writing, and in how they answer "who are you?" — a Quin'dhel names an ancestor, a Gla'mdroi names a hold.

## Where elves come from, as elves tell it

The elves have no maker and no origin story, and they consider this a mark of age — young peoples have origin myths; old things simply were. What every elf holds is this:

- **Sidherun means "the Spirits' Road."** The old elven name for the continent. The road is the path spirits walk between the world and what lies beneath it. No one made it, and the gods remember no beginning of it.
- **The elves are the spirits who stopped.** The sidhe were travelers on the road. Some heard the Grove's song and stopped, growing bodies as trees grow. The first to root in the Grove founded the lines the Quin'dhel still count descent by; the Gla'mdroi descend from those who never wholly stopped, for whom the road still matters more than the ancestor.
- **No elf remembers the stopping.** The memory-people, whose one verb *meor-* covers both knowing and remembering, cannot remember their own beginning. Every elf knows the First Song is younger than the stopping it describes.
- **A stopped spirit stands outside the flow of life and death.** This is why elves live so long — the current barely tugs them — and why, in the stories, both Vælloria and Morvathun treat elves as natural neutrals on the boundary.
- **Death is the road resumed.** The elven verb for dying is *seth-*, "grow still." When an elf grows still, the spirit steps back onto the road. Some tellings say the road returns in the last breath — the only moment an elf remembers it. Morvathun does not hurry the elves; they are travelers of his road, overdue by choice.

## The First Song

The **Eth'lae** is the origin liturgy of both kindreds — sung at namings, at deaths, and on the last night of autumn. It is never written. Both kindreds refuse to trust it to marks or knots; committing the song to a record is held to be a kind of killing it. It lives in memory, and memory is the thing elves hold higher than any archive.

The full song, with translation, is at [`languages/texts/eth-lae.md`](../languages/texts/eth-lae.md). An elf character knows all thirteen stanzas by heart.

## Those who walk again

Death is the road resumed, and walking back is the one violation. The dead who return to the world — the **anu'runorin**, "those who walk again" — are neither spirits nor kin, and the Sidhe'dhel hold halting them as a duty older than either kindred. The song says it plainly: *Ea dar nin'tar* — "stopping is our burden." This is why elven tradition is implacable toward the returned dead in every form.

The Eth'lae's warning verses (stanzas 10–11) name one more thing: a walker on the road who never stopped and never will, whom the elves call **Aia'runor**, the Always-Walker. He un-stops the dead, and the anu'runorin are his. The song says nothing else about him, and the elves consider that prudence.

## Speech

The elven language is **Quin'lae** ("grove-song"), shared by both kindreds. It has two registers:

- **Eth'vor** ("first-speech") — the formal voice: liturgy, oaths, diplomacy, and any telling of a story. In ceremony the Sidhe'dhel are one voice; both kindreds sing and swear identically.
- **Din'vor** ("day-speech") — everyday talk, and the place the kindreds part. Quin'dhel day-speech stays close to the formal register, so humans who learn "Elvish" from Quin'dhel teachers come away sounding formal. Gla'mdroi day-speech clips particles and runs words together at the seams (*ve'thu* for *veth thu*, "with you").

Elves count in twelves. A dozen is a *sil*, "a moon," because a year holds twelve of them; 144 is *silsil*, "a moon of moons."

Common table phrases, in both registers, are collected in [`languages/texts/phrasebook.md`](../languages/texts/phrasebook.md).

## Writing

The Sidhe'dhel speak one language and write two:

- **Thal'threl** ("tree-marks") — the ancestral script of all elves: straight strokes with branching finials, cut into living wood so the marks grow with the tree. The Gla'mdroi still cut it for hold-boundaries, way-marks, grave-trees, and treaty-trees.
- **Syl'vor** ("weave-speech") — the Quin'dhel hand. The Grove-kin stopped cutting trees to write on them; their records are knotted cords of dyed thread, kept and read by the weaver-healers. Knot-order carries the words, color carries the register.

Each kindred can slowly puzzle out the other's hand, but fluency in both is rare and marks a scholar or a go-between. The oldest Thal'threl inscriptions predate the parting of the kindreds and are written in the elder speech beneath Quin'lae; no living elf reads them fully, though a Gla'mdroi elder can piece out fragments. Travelers tell of the ironbark trees above Duskmantle Hollow, whose shadows look like writing in a language that no longer exists — what they half-see is the look of old Thal'threl.

The one record neither script is trusted with is the Eth'lae.

## Names

Elven names join two roots with an apostrophe, modifier first: Quin'Tiel is "Grove-Gate," Gla'mDroia "the Wild-Hold." Given names are a root plus a gender-neutral suffix (-iel, -ael, -is, -eth, -ai, -wen, -orin): Thaleth, Silwen, Veriel, Aelis. Formal style adds origin: *Thaleth of Qu'Droia*. Elves of mixed descent keep a human surname.

## The gods, in elven speech

Elves invoke the gods by Quin'lae names:

| Quin'lae name | Gloss | Deity |
|---|---|---|
| Qaal'el | The Merciful Light | Selinthræ |
| Ver'ama | Life-Mother | Vælloria |
| Mor'el | The Still Light | Morvathun |
| Ith'ver | The Deep Green | Roughnira |
| Seth'wen | The Still Wind | Serakhala |

"Trust Qaal'el's mercy" is an elven blessing.

The story every elf knows of the two gods is **Tem'wen**, "The Equinox of Breath" — the meeting of Ver'ama and Mor'el in the Deep Grove, told in full at [`languages/texts/tem-wen.md`](../languages/texts/tem-wen.md).
