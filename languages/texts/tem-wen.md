# Tem'wen — "The Equinox of Breath" in Quin'lae

<!-- source: translated from the World Story "The Equinox of Breath" (Sidherun Notion, World Stories); first Quin'lae text, 2026-07-18 -->

The first full text in Quin'lae, translated from the World Story "The Equinox of Breath" as a compressed elven telling — every story beat, not every English sentence. Grammar and lexicon per [`../quindel.md`](../quindel.md) §11–§12 (VSO word order; stanzas 7 and 20 use the v2.1 object pronouns).

Format per stanza: Quin'lae, then word-by-word gloss, then plain English.

---

1. **Nu halir quin len im venin veren. Vorir meorin al o El'sae: «Ith'quin.» Aia eir sa don o elanin dath.**
   not held grove name in mouths living · said records(memories) old of Lightwells: Deep-Grove · always was it place of gods two
   The grove had no name in living mouths. The old records of the Lightwells called it the Deep Grove. It had always been the meeting-place of the two gods.

2. **Tolir Caelindra im eth, Ver'laeor veren. Runir sa maen dinin ter thalin an don.**
   came Caelindra in first, life-singer young · walked she many days through trees to the-place
   Caelindra came first, a young Pulse-Singer. She walked many days through the trees to reach it.

3. **Eir thalin nu veren ar nu moren, ar eir don al.**
   were trees not living and not dead, and was ground old
   The trees were neither living nor dead, and the ground was old.

4. **Donir sa davenin: senin veren, thal'laen, ar thal moren. Nara Tem'wen ver ar mor.**
   placed she offerings: seeds living, tree-water(sap), and wood dead · needs Breath-Balance life and death
   She set down the offerings: living seeds, sap, dead wood. The Equinox requires both life and death.

5. **Vorir sa'ama, El'halor Hesswyn: «Thiruin thu vira o wen. Im eth lath — ea sa Ver'ama. Ono, seth — ea sa Mor'el. Nu thir thren. Ea dath naren.»**
   said her-elder, Light-Keeper Hesswyn: will-feel you turning of air · in first scent — is that Life-Mother · after, silence — is that Still-Light · not feel fear · are the-two needed
   Her elder, Lumen-Keeper Hesswyn, had told her: "You will feel the air turn. First the scent — that is Her. Then the silence — that is Him. Do not be afraid. Both are necessary."

6. **Elir sa soril. Virir sor, ar nu eir wen.**
   lit she little-flame(candle) · turned the-flame, and not was wind
   She lit the candle. The flame wavered, though there was no wind.

7. **Thirir sa Ver'ama enu velir sa ansa: lath o don ar laen, ar verilin nim ya verir, nuverir, ar verir anu.**
   felt she Life-Mother before saw she her(obj) · scent of earth and water, and flowers white that bloomed, faded, and bloomed again
   She felt Vælloria before she saw her: the scent of soil and rain, and white flowers that bloomed, faded, and bloomed again.

8. **Nu tolir elan; verir sa, sylen os ver o quin.**
   not arrived the-god; grew she, woven from the-life of the-grove
   The goddess did not arrive; she grew, woven from the living pulse of the grove.

9. **«Ea thu hael os thu'quin,» vorir Ver'ama. «Run hael an Ver'laeor veren.»**
   are you far from your-grove, said Life-Mother · road far for life-singer young
   "You are far from your grove," said Vælloria. "A long road for a young Pulse-Singer."

10. **«Ea El'halor nuveren,» vorir Caelindra. «Querir sa ni tar daven.»**
    is Light-Keeper fading, said Caelindra · asked she me carry offering
    "The Lumen-Keeper is fading," said Caelindra. "She asked me to carry the offering."

11. **«Na. Meora ni.»**
    yes · know/remember I
    "Yes. I know." (In Quin'lae, to know and to remember are the same verb.)

12. **Ono nuelir soril, ar eir seth im don o el.**
    after went-dark the-candle, and was silence in the-place of the-light
    Then the candle went out, and where the light had been there was silence.

13. **Nu runir Mor'el an quin. Eir sa.**
    not walked Still-Light to grove · was he
    Morvathun did not walk into the grove. He simply was.

14. **Eir sa duth ar orin al. Thirir Caelindra sul min ya dona sa'tar al.**
    was he shadow and stone old · felt Caelindra like one who sets-down their-load long-carried
    He was shadow and old stone. Caelindra felt like one who sets down a burden carried too long.

15. **Vorir sa: «El'halor.» Querir Caelindra. «Nu,» vorir sa. Eir sa'vor tan, sul sorun.**
    said he: Light-Keeper · asked Caelindra · no, said he · was his-word certain, like the-year
    "The Lumen-Keeper," he said. She asked what she had come to ask. "No," he said — certain, the way the year is certain.

16. **Vorir Ver'ama: «Sylir sa sa'quin maen sorunin. Methuin sa im qaal.» «Methuin sa,» vorir Mor'el. Ar eir sa'vor qaal.**
    said Life-Mother: tended she her-grove many years · will-rest she in mercy · will-rest she, said Still-Light · and was his-word mercy
    Vælloria said: "She has tended her grove many years. She will rest in grace." "She will," said Morvathun. And in his voice, that was comfort.

17. **Donir Caelindra daven methen: veril moren, ya halir sa'ama maen sorunin. Velir Ver'ama veth dael. Nu vorir Mor'el, ar eir sa'seth qaal.**
    placed Caelindra offering last: flower dead, that kept her-elder many years · looked Life-Mother with gratitude · not spoke Still-Light, and was his-silence mercy
    Caelindra set down one last offering: a dried flower her elder had kept for many years. Vælloria looked on it with gratitude. Morvathun said nothing, and his stillness was its own tenderness.

18. **Elir sa soril anu. Halir sor.**
    lit she candle again · held the-flame
    She re-lit the candle. The flame held.

19. **Sethir El'halor ono nel dinin, im dineth o Tem'wen, veth tielil laden ar lath o quin.**
    grew-still Light-Keeper after three days, on morning of Breath-Balance, with little-gate(window) open and scent of grove
    The Lumen-Keeper died three days later, on the morning of the Equinox, her window open to the scent of the grove.

20. **Verir verilin nim im sa'don. Verir, nuverir, ar verir anu. Nu senir min ansain.**
    bloomed flowers white at her-place · bloomed, faded, and bloomed again · not planted anyone them(obj)
    White flowers bloomed at her bedside. They bloomed, faded, and bloomed again. No one had planted them.

21. **Im virun aia, dona laeorin o elanin dath davenin im tielin: sen im don o mor. Hala sain seth.**
    in autumn always, place singers of gods two offerings at thresholds: seed in place of death · hold they silence
    Each autumn, the clergy of both gods leave offerings at shared thresholds — seed beside ash — and keep silence.
