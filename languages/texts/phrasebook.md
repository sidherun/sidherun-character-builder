# Quin'lae Table Phrasebook

<!-- source: coined from languages/quindel.md §11–§12 (grammar v2.2); first use of the register ruling; authored 2026-07-18 -->

Stock utterances a GM can drop into play without deriving grammar at the table. Everything here follows [`../quindel.md`](../quindel.md) — VSO, the v2.1 question words and conditionals, and the v2.2 registers. Five small coinages ship with this file and are logged in §12: **dar-** (halt, stay), **haeldhel** (stranger, "far-kin"), **quaen** (how many), plus *dhel* promoted to a freestanding noun (kinsman; in day-speech, a friend) and *tem* extended to "peace, truce".

Register key: entries are **Din'vor** (day-speech) unless marked; **Eth'vor** is the formal voice. Where the kindreds differ, the Gla'mdroi form shows the liaison habit. Pronunciation crib: qu = kw, dh = the *th* of "this", stress the first syllable of the last root.

## Challenges and warnings

| Quin'lae | Literal | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dar!** | halt! | Halt! | Both registers; the sentry's bark. |
| **Dar! Quon runa?** | halt! who walks? | Halt — who goes there? | |
| **Quath thu'len?** | what your-name? | What is your name? | Eth'vor: *Quath ea thu'len?* |
| **Quor thu'droia?** | where your-hold? | Where are you from? | Elves ask for your hold, not your country. Eth'vor: *Quor ea thu'droia?* |
| **Ea thu haeldhel im nin'don.** | are you far-kin in our-place | You are a stranger here. | Eth'vor; a border formality, spoken before either welcome or refusal. |
| **Nu run ter Ith'quin.** | not walk through Deep-Grove | Do not go through the Shadowgrove. | Negative command: *nu* + bare root. |
| **Thren im thalin.** | fear in the-trees | There is danger in the woods. | Copula dropped; Eth'vor: *Ea thren im thalin.* |
| **Sor im thalin!** | flame in the-trees! | Fire in the woods! | |
| **Run os!** | walk from! | Be gone. | Blunt Din'vor. A Quin'dhel would say *Run os nin'don* — "walk from our place." |
| **Hal anni!** | hold me! | Help me! | *hal-* "hold, keep" doubles as "help"; to help someone is to hold them. |

## Greetings and partings

| Quin'lae | Literal | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Qaal an thu.** | grace to you | Hello (formal or first meeting). | Gla'mdroi liaison: *Qaal a'thu.* |
| **Don laden.** | place open | Welcome. | Host to guest at a threshold: "the place is open." |
| **Run veth el.** | walk with light | Farewell (to one departing). | Gla'mdroi liaison: *Run ve'el.* |
| **Hala quin anthu.** | holds the-grove you | The grove keep you. | Eth'vor farewell, solemn. The everyday clip is just *Hala quin.* |
| **Meth im qaal.** | rest in grace | Goodnight. | The same words Vælloria speaks over the dying in Tem'wen 16 — said nightly without weight. |
| **Dael an thu.** | gratitude to you | Thank you. | Din'vor: *Dael.* |

## Blessings and curses

| Quin'lae | Literal | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Veruin thu, nuveruin thu, veruin thu anu.** | will-bloom you, will-fade you, will-bloom you again | Blessing over the sick or grieving. | The Tem'wen flower line (stanzas 7, 20) turned toward a person. |
| **Nu meoruin min anthu.** | not will-remember anyone you | No one will remember you. | The worst Quin'lae curse. *meor-* means both know and remember, so this promises erasure. |
| **Nu veluin thu quin anu.** | not will-see you the-grove again | You will never see the grove again. | The exile's sentence; also muttered as a curse. |
| **Ea thu dhel o Hul.** | are you kin of the-Hul | You are Hul-kin. | An accusation of falseness — the Hul wear elven spelling without the speech (§9). Din'vor: *Thu dhel'Hul.* |

## Table talk

| Quin'lae | Literal | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quaen dinin an Quin'Tiel?** | how-many days to Grove-Gate? | How many days to Quin'Tiel? | Answer: *Nel.* — three. Numbers in §11 (base-12). |
| **Quen runa thu ter tiel, tas sethuin thu.** | if walk you through the-gate, then will-grow-still you | Pass the gate and you die. | Conditional threat; *seth-* "grow still" is the elven verb for dying. |
| **Que meora thu Quin'lae?** | (question) know you grove-song? | Do you speak Quin'lae? | To speak a language is to remember it. Din'vor drops *que*: *Meora thu Quin'lae?* |
| **Na. / Nu.** | yes / no | Yes. / No. | |
| **Donen.** | set-down | Done; agreed. | The participle of *don-* "set down" — a bargain sealed by setting it on the ground. |
